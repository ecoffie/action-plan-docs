# Federal Contracting Roadmap

## Overview
This roadmap provides a structured approach to entering and succeeding in federal contracting. It's organized into five phases that guide businesses from initial setup through contract management.

## Phase 1: Setup (Once)

**Objective:** Establish the foundational requirements for federal contracting

### Key Activities:
- **Choose your Business Structure**
  - Supplier v Service Provider v Consultant
  - Understand DUNS and UEI
  - Creating Professional Email

- **Identify your Industry codes (NAICS)**
  - How to Identify NAICS codes
  - Selecting appropriate codes for your business

- **Create your SAM.gov Profile**
  - How to Create a SAM.gov Profile
  - Complete entity registration
  - DSBS registration

- **Talk to Local Apex Accelerator**
  - Benefits of Apex Accelerator
  - What to say to Apex Accelerator
  - Accessing local resources and support

- **Create/Fix your Business Resume (Capability Statement)**
  - What to put on Capability Statement
  - Key differentiators
  - Clip from Bootcamp - differentiators

## Phase 2: Bidding (Repeat)

**Objective:** Respond to opportunities and submit competitive proposals

### Key Activities:
- **Review Immediate Bid Opportunities**
  - How to Find Bid Opportunities
  - Understanding different opportunity types
  - Opportunity evaluation criteria

- **Assemble Team Based on Opportunities**
  - Identifying required capabilities
  - Partner and subcontractor selection
  - Team formation strategies

- **Apply for Vendor/Supplier Credit**
  - Understanding credit requirements
  - Preparing credit applications
  - Building financial credibility

- **Respond to Opportunity (RFP, RFQ, RFI, Task Orders)**
  - Understanding solicitation types
  - Proposal development process
  - Submission requirements and deadlines

- **Evaluate Bid Results**
  - Reviewing award outcomes
  - Requesting debriefings
  - Learning from wins and losses

## Phase 3: Business Development (Repeat)

**Objective:** Build relationships and identify long-term opportunities

### Key Activities:
- **Identify Top 25 Buyers & Future Bids (NOT ON SAM)**
  - Researching agency spending patterns
  - Identifying key decision makers
  - Finding opportunities before they're posted

- **Setup and attend meetings with government buyers**
  - Reaching out to contracting officers
  - Scheduling capability briefings
  - Building relationships

- **Attend Industry Events**
  - Industry days and trade shows
  - Networking opportunities
  - Learning about upcoming opportunities

- **Attend Site Visits**
  - Facility tours and site visits
  - Understanding customer needs
  - Demonstrating capabilities

- **Get on Supplier List for top 25 Federal Suppliers**
  - Teaming opportunities
  - Subcontracting networks
  - Prime contractor relationships

- **Monitor Contract Awards and Identify Sub Opportunities**
  - Track long term contracts to bid (IDIQ)
  - Subcontracting opportunities
  - Ongoing pipeline development

## Phase 4: Business Enhancement (Once)

**Objective:** Strengthen competitive position and growth capabilities

### Key Activities:
- **Apply for Small Business Certification**
  - Understanding certification options
  - Eligibility requirements
  - Application process

- **8(a) Certification**
  - Program benefits
  - Eligibility and application
  - Maximizing 8(a) advantages

- **Mentor Protégé Program**
  - Finding mentors
  - Program benefits
  - Building strategic relationships

- **Focus on Self Performance Capability as Differentiator**
  - Demonstrating past performance
  - Building capability statements
  - Showcasing unique strengths

- **Find Better Partners**
  - Strategic partnerships
  - Joint venture opportunities
  - Complementary capabilities

- **Identify Mid Size Mentor**
  - Mentor-protégé relationships
  - Learning from experienced contractors
  - Accessing larger opportunities

- **Speak at an event**
  - Building thought leadership
  - Industry recognition
  - Networking and visibility

## Phase 5: Contract Management

**Objective:** Successfully execute contracts and maintain performance

### Key Activities:
- **System Registrations (PIEE, WAWF)**
  - PIEE registration for invoicing
  - WAWF (Wide Area Workflow) setup
  - System access and training

- **Subcontractor Compliance**
  - Subcontractor management
  - Compliance monitoring
  - Reporting requirements

- **Project Compliance**
  - Contract terms and conditions
  - Performance requirements
  - Quality standards

- **Communication**
  - Regular reporting
  - Contracting officer relations
  - Issue resolution

---

## Phase Categories

- **Once:** Phases that are typically completed once (Phase 1 and Phase 4)
- **Repeat:** Phases that are ongoing and repeatable (Phase 2 and Phase 3)

## Success Factors
- Complete Phase 1 thoroughly before moving forward
- Continuously cycle through Phase 2 and Phase 3
- Invest in Phase 4 enhancements strategically
- Maintain excellence in Phase 5 for future opportunities
