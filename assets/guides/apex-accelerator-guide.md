# Apex Accelerator Guide

## What is an Apex Accelerator?

Apex Accelerators (formerly known as Procurement Technical Assistance Centers or PTACs) are part of a nationwide network of organizations that provide free or low-cost assistance to businesses interested in selling products and services to federal, state, and local governments.

## Benefits of Apex Accelerators

### Free or Low-Cost Services
- **Counseling**: One-on-one counseling sessions with procurement specialists
- **Training**: Workshops and webinars on federal contracting topics
- **Bid Matching**: Automated matching of opportunities to your business profile
- **Proposal Review**: Review and feedback on proposals before submission
- **Market Research**: Help identifying relevant opportunities and agencies

### Access to Resources
- **Contracting Officer Connections**: Relationships with local contracting officers
- **Small Business Specialists**: Access to agency small business specialists
- **Industry Days**: Information about upcoming industry days and events
- **Teaming Opportunities**: Connections to potential teaming partners
- **Resource Library**: Templates, guides, and reference materials

### Expertise
- **Knowledgeable Staff**: Certified procurement counselors
- **Current Information**: Up-to-date on federal contracting requirements
- **Best Practices**: Proven strategies and approaches
- **Problem Solving**: Help navigating challenges and obstacles

## Finding Your Local Apex Accelerator

### Search Methods
1. **APTAC Website**: https://www.aptac-us.org
   - Use the "Find a PTAC" locator tool
   - Enter your location or zip code
   - View contact information and services

2. **SBA Resource Partners**: Small Business Administration website
   - SBA district offices often have PTAC information
   - Local SBA resource partner network

3. **State Economic Development Offices**
   - Many PTACs are part of state economic development networks

4. **Local Chambers of Commerce**
   - Often have partnerships with PTACs
   - Can provide referrals

## What to Say to Apex Accelerator

### Initial Contact Email/Phone Script

**Subject**: New Business Seeking Federal Contracting Assistance

**Message:**
"Hello,

My name is [Your Name], and I'm the [Title] at [Company Name]. We're a [Type of Business - Small Business, Woman-Owned, etc.] located in [City, State].

We're interested in entering the federal contracting marketplace and understand that Apex Accelerator provides assistance to businesses like ours. 

We're particularly interested in:
- [Specific area of interest - e.g., "understanding SAM.gov registration"]
- [Specific area of interest - e.g., "finding opportunities in [industry/NAICS code]"]
- [Specific area of interest - e.g., "learning about proposal development"]

Could we schedule an initial consultation to discuss how Apex Accelerator might be able to help us?

Thank you for your time.

Best regards,
[Your Name]
[Company Name]
[Contact Information]"

### First Meeting Preparation

**Before the Meeting:**
- [ ] Have your business information ready (EIN, NAICS codes if known)
- [ ] Prepare a brief description of your business and capabilities
- [ ] List specific questions or areas where you need help
- [ ] Bring a capability statement if you have one
- [ ] Bring business cards

**Questions to Ask:**
1. "What services does Apex Accelerator provide?"
2. "How often can we meet with a counselor?"
3. "Do you offer bid matching services?"
4. "Can you help us with proposal reviews?"
5. "What training workshops do you offer?"
6. "How do you help businesses connect with contracting officers?"
7. "What should be our first steps in federal contracting?"
8. "Are there any fees for your services?"

### During the Consultation

**Be Prepared to Discuss:**
- Your business background and experience
- Products or services you offer
- Your goals for federal contracting
- Current challenges or questions
- Timeline expectations

**What to Expect:**
- Counselor will ask about your business
- They'll assess where you are in the process
- They'll provide guidance on next steps
- They may set up follow-up appointments
- They'll explain available services

### Following Up

**After the Initial Meeting:**
- [ ] Send a thank you email
- [ ] Schedule follow-up appointments as recommended
- [ ] Complete any action items discussed
- [ ] Register for any recommended training
- [ ] Prepare requested documentation

**Ongoing Relationship:**
- Regular check-ins with your counselor
- Attend training workshops and events
- Utilize bid matching services
- Request proposal reviews
- Stay engaged and proactive

## Maximizing Your Apex Accelerator Relationship

### Do's
- ✅ Come prepared to meetings
- ✅ Be open and honest about your challenges
- ✅ Follow through on action items
- ✅ Attend training and workshops
- ✅ Provide feedback on services
- ✅ Share your successes and challenges
- ✅ Utilize all available resources

### Don'ts
- ❌ Expect them to write your proposals for you
- ❌ Rely solely on them for all your federal contracting needs
- ❌ Be passive - take initiative
- ❌ Miss appointments without notice
- ❌ Ignore their advice and recommendations

## Typical Services Timeline

### Initial Phase (Months 1-3)
- Initial consultation and assessment
- SAM.gov registration assistance
- NAICS code selection guidance
- Basic training workshops
- Capability statement development

### Development Phase (Months 4-12)
- Bid matching and opportunity identification
- Proposal development training
- One-on-one proposal reviews
- Market research assistance
- Introductions to contracting officers

### Growth Phase (Ongoing)
- Advanced training and workshops
- Strategic business development guidance
- Certification assistance
- Teaming and partnership facilitation
- Performance management support
