### Understanding DSBS (10 minutes)

**What is DSBS?**
- Dynamic Small Business Search
- Public, searchable database of small businesses
- Maintained by the Small Business Administration (SBA)
- Free for small businesses to register
- Free for buyers to search

**Purpose and Function:**
- **Small Business Discovery Tool**: Helps contracting officers find capable small businesses
- **Agency Market Research**: Used by agencies for Sources Sought and market research
- **Prime Contractor Searches**: Primes use it to find subcontractors
- **Opportunity Matching**: Can help match businesses to opportunities

**Who Uses DSBS?**
- **Federal Contracting Officers**: Finding small businesses for set-asides and opportunities
- **Prime Contractors**: Finding subcontractors for teaming and subcontracting
- **Subcontracting Specialists**: Identifying small business partners
- **Small Business Specialists**: Market research and outreach
- **Market Researchers**: Understanding small business landscape

**Key Insight:** "If you're not in DSBS, you're invisible to many buyers who use it as their primary search tool."

### DSBS Registration Process (15 minutes)

**Prerequisites:**
- **SAM.gov Registration Required**: Must complete SAM.gov registration first
- **Small Business Status**: Must qualify as small business
- **Business Information Ready**: Have all business details prepared

**Step-by-Step Registration:**

**Step 1: Access DSBS**
- Go to SBA.gov/DSBS or search "DSBS SBA"
- Click on "Create/Update DSBS Profile" or "Register Your Business"
- Use your SAM.gov login credentials (if possible) or create account

**Step 2: Create DSBS Profile**
- Enter your UEI (Unique Entity Identifier) from SAM.gov
- Verify your business information
- Confirm small business status
- Accept terms and conditions

**Step 3: Enter Business Information**
- Business name (matches SAM.gov)
- Business address
- Contact information
- Website (if applicable)
- Business description

**Step 4: Complete Profile Sections**
- **NAICS Codes**: Select your primary NAICS codes (matches SAM.gov)
- **Capabilities Description**: Detailed description of what you do
- **Past Performance**: Add relevant projects/contracts
- **Certifications**: List small business certifications
- **Geographic Service Areas**: Where you can perform work

**Step 5: Submit Registration**
- Review all information for accuracy
- Submit profile
- Receive confirmation
- Profile goes live (usually immediately or within 24 hours)

**Profile Information Required:**

**Basic Business Information:**
- Legal business name
- DBA (if applicable)
- Physical address
- Mailing address (if different)
- Phone number
- Email address
- Website URL

**NAICS Codes:**
- Primary NAICS codes (from SAM.gov)
- Secondary NAICS codes
- Ensure codes match your capabilities
- Select codes relevant to your services/products

**Capabilities Description:**
- Clear description of what you do
- Key services or products
- Industry focus areas
- Unique capabilities
- Use keywords buyers might search

**Contact Information:**
- Primary contact person
- Title
- Phone number
- Email address
- Preferred contact method

**Past Performance (Optional but Recommended):**
- Relevant projects or contracts
- Client names (if allowed)
- Contract values
- Key accomplishments
- Results and outcomes

**Certifications:**
- Small business certifications (8(a), WOSB, HUBZone, VOSB, SDVOSB)
- Industry certifications
- Quality certifications (ISO, etc.)
- Professional certifications
