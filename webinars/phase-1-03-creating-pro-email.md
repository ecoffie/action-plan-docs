### Professional Email Domain (15 minutes)

**Why a Custom Domain?**

**Credibility and Professionalism:**
- Shows you're a real business, not just someone with a personal Gmail
- Demonstrates investment in your business
- Creates professional first impression
- Builds trust with government buyers

**Brand Consistency:**
- Your domain = your brand
- Consistent email addresses across organization
- Professional appearance in all communications
- Memorable and recognizable

**Trust Factor:**
- Free email (Gmail, Yahoo, Hotmail) can look like sole proprietor or hobbyist
- Custom domain shows you're established
- Government buyers take you more seriously
- Avoids looking like a part-time operation

**Avoid Looking Like a Sole Proprietor:**
- Even if you're a solo operation, professional email helps
- Shows you're treating this as a real business
- Separates personal from professional
- Essential for federal contracting credibility

**Setting Up Email Domain:**

**Domain Registration Options:**
- GoDaddy, Namecheap, Google Domains, etc.
- Typically $10-15 per year for .com domain
- Choose domain that matches your business name
- Keep it simple and professional

**Email Hosting Services:**

**Google Workspace (formerly G Suite):**
- Professional Gmail with your domain
- $6-18 per user per month
- Includes Google Drive, Calendar, Docs
- Familiar Gmail interface
- Good for small businesses

**Microsoft 365:**
- Outlook email with your domain
- $6-22 per user per month
- Includes Office apps, OneDrive, Teams
- Professional Microsoft ecosystem
- Good for businesses using Office

**Other Hosting Options:**
- Many domain registrars offer email hosting
- Some web hosting includes email
- Cost varies, some as low as $2-5/month
- Check features and storage limits

**Cost Considerations:**
- Domain: $10-15/year (one-time per year)
- Email hosting: $60-200/year per user
- Worth the investment for professional appearance
- Consider as essential business expense

**Domain Best Practices:**

**Choose a Professional Domain Name:**
- Match your business name exactly if possible
- Keep it simple and memorable
- Easy to spell and pronounce
- Avoid confusing spellings

**Match Your Business Name:**
- Use your exact legal business name if available
- Or close variation if exact match taken
- Consistency across all communications
- Easy for buyers to remember

**Keep it Simple and Memorable:**
- Shorter is better
- Easy to type
- Easy to say over the phone
- Avoid unnecessary words

**Avoid Hyphens and Numbers:**
- Hard to communicate verbally
- Looks unprofessional
- Confusing in email addresses
- Choose clean, simple domain

### Email Address Structure (10 minutes)

**Standard Formats:**

**firstname.lastname@company.com:**
- Professional and clear
- Easy to identify sender
- Common corporate format
- Good for smaller teams

**firstinitial.lastname@company.com:**
- Shorter alternative
- Good if full name is long
- Still professional
- Common format

**info@company.com (general):**
- For general inquiries
- Public-facing address
- Multiple people can monitor
- Professional inquiry address

**contact@company.com (general):**
- Alternative to info@
- General contact address
- Customer service inquiries
- Public contact point

**Role-Based Addresses:**

**info@company.com:**
- General business inquiries
- Public contact point
- Website contact form
- Marketing communications

**contact@company.com:**
- Alternative contact address
- Customer service
- General inquiries
- Public-facing

**proposals@company.com:**
- Proposal submissions
- Opportunity responses
- RFP/RFQ submissions
- Proposal team access

**accounting@company.com:**
- Financial matters
- Invoicing
- Payment questions
- Accounting department

**legal@company.com:**
- Legal matters
- Contracts
- Compliance questions
- Legal department

**Setting Up Multiple Addresses:**

**Individual Addresses for Key Personnel:**
- CEO/Owner email
- Operations manager
- Proposal manager
- Key contacts for agencies
- Professional individual identity

**Shared/Group Addresses for Departments:**
- Multiple people can access
- Shared inbox
- Department coordination
- Team communications

**Forwarding and Aliases:**
- Forward info@ to personal email initially
- Set up aliases for different purposes
- Multiple addresses point to same inbox
- Cost-effective for small businesses

**Organization Structure:**
- Plan your email structure early
- Consistent naming conventions
- Scalable as business grows
- Professional organization

### Professional Email Signatures (8 minutes)

**What to Include:**

**Essential Elements:**
- **Name and Title**: Full name, job title
- **Company Name**: Your business name
- **Phone Number**: Business phone number
- **Email Address**: The email you're sending from
- **Website URL**: Your business website (if you have one)

**Recommended Additions:**
- **Physical Address**: Business address (optional but professional)
- **UEI Number**: Unique Entity Identifier (very helpful for federal contracting)
- **CAGE Code**: If you have one
- **Social Media**: LinkedIn profile (if professional)

**Formatting Best Practices:**

**Clean and Professional:**
- Simple, readable font
- Consistent formatting
- Not too many colors
- Professional appearance
- Mobile-friendly

**Consistent Across Organization:**
- Same format for all employees
- Brand consistency
- Professional appearance
- Unified image

**Not Too Long:**
- Keep it concise
- Essential information only
- 4-6 lines maximum
- Don't overwhelm signature

**Mobile-Friendly:**
- Test on mobile devices
- Keep formatting simple
- Avoid complex tables
- Ensure readability

**Include Social Media (if appropriate):**
- LinkedIn (professional networking)
- Company website
- Avoid personal social media
- Keep it business-focused

**Example Signatures:**

**CEO/Owner Signature:**

### Email Organization for Federal Contracting (5 minutes)

**Organizing Incoming Emails:**

**Folders by Opportunity:**
- Create folder for each opportunity
- Store all related emails
- Easy to find information
- Track communication history
- Proposal organization

**Folders by Agency:**
- Organize by federal agency
- Track agency communications
- Build agency relationships
- Easy reference for future opportunities

**Folders by Phase/Topic:**
- Phase 1: Setup
- Phase 2: Bidding
- Phase 3: Business Development
- Phase 4: Enhancement
- Phase 5: Contract Management

**Automated Filtering Rules:**
- Set up rules for common senders
- Automatically file emails
- Reduce inbox clutter
- Improve organization
- Save time

**Managing Outgoing Communications:**

**Templates for Common Communications:**
- Introduction emails to contracting officers
- Capability briefing requests
- Follow-up emails
- Thank you emails
- Proposal submission emails
- Save time and maintain consistency

**Tracking Sent Emails:**
- Keep records of sent emails
- Track follow-up dates
- Monitor response rates
- Document communications
- Important for relationship building

**Follow-up Systems:**
- Set reminders for follow-ups
- Track when to follow up
- Don't let emails fall through cracks
- Consistent follow-up builds relationships
- Use calendar reminders

**Contact Database Integration:**
- Link emails to contact database
- Track communications with contacts
- Build relationship history
- CRM integration (if using)
- Centralized contact management

### Best Practices (2 minutes)

**Communication Guidelines:**

**Professional Tone:**
- Business-appropriate language
- Respectful and courteous
- Clear and concise
- Professional but personable
- Avoid slang or casual language

**Timely Responses:**
- Respond within 24-48 hours
- Faster for urgent matters
- Shows professionalism
- Builds trust
- Demonstrates reliability

**Clear Subject Lines:**
- Descriptive subject lines
- Include opportunity number if relevant
- Make it easy to identify
- Help recipients prioritize
- Improve open rates

**Proper Grammar and Spelling:**
- Proofread before sending
- Use spell check
- Professional writing
- Avoid typos
- Shows attention to detail

**Attachment Naming Conventions:**
- Use descriptive file names
- Include date or version number
- Include company name or opportunity number
- Professional file names
- Easy to identify and organize
