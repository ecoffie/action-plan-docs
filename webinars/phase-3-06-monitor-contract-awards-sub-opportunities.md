# Webinar: Monitor Contract Awards and Identify Sub Opportunities

**Phase:** Phase 3 - Business Development (Repeat)  
**Duration:** 45-60 minutes  
**Level:** Intermediate  
**Prerequisites:** Understanding of subcontracting

## Learning Objectives
By the end of this webinar, participants will be able to:
- Track contract awards effectively
- Identify subcontracting opportunities
- Understand IDIQ and long-term contracts
- Find subcontracting networks
- Position for subcontracting opportunities

## Agenda

### Introduction (5 minutes)
- Welcome and context
- Subcontracting as growth strategy
- Opportunity pipeline

### Tracking Contract Awards (15 minutes)
- **Sources for Award Information**
  - USAspending.gov
  - FPDS
  - SAM.gov (Contract Opportunities)
  - Agency announcements
  
- **What to Monitor**
  - IDIQ awards
  - Multiple award contracts (MAC)
  - Large contract awards
  - Agency contracts
  - Relevant NAICS codes

### IDIQ and Long-Term Contracts (15 minutes)
- **Understanding IDIQs**
  - Indefinite Delivery, Indefinite Quantity
  - Multiple award vehicles
  - Task order opportunities
  - Long-term potential
  
- **Tracking IDIQs**
  - Award announcements
  - Holder identification
  - Task order monitoring
  - Teaming opportunities

### Finding Subcontracting Opportunities (12 minutes)
- **SubNet**
  - SBA subcontracting network
  - Prime contractor postings
  - Opportunity search
  
- **Prime Contractor Portals**
  - Supplier portals
  - Opportunity postings
  - RFQ notifications
  
- **Direct Outreach**
  - Contacting primes
  - Capability statements
  - Relationship building

### Positioning for Opportunities (8 minutes)
- **Preparedness**
  - Capability statements ready
  - Past performance documented
  - Quick response capability
  - Relationships established

## Key Takeaways
1. Continuous monitoring identifies subcontracting opportunities
2. IDIQ contracts provide ongoing task order opportunities
3. Multiple sources exist for finding sub opportunities
4. Preparedness enables quick response
5. Relationships with primes facilitate opportunities
