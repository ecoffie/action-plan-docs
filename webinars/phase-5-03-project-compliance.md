## Presentation Notes

### Opening (5 minutes)
**Welcome and Context:**
- "Welcome to Project Compliance. You've won the contract. Now you have to deliver—and deliver in a way that meets every requirement. That's project compliance."
- "Government contracts have specific terms, deliverables, and quality standards. Miss them, and you risk cure notices, contract termination, or worse: a bad CPARS rating that follows you for years."
- "Today we'll cover how to understand your contract terms, meet performance requirements, and stay compliant from day one to closeout."

**Project Compliance Importance:**
- Compliance protects your contract and your reputation
- CPARS (Contractor Performance Assessment) affects future bids—past performance matters
- Deliverable acceptance is often required before payment
- Non-compliance can lead to cure notices, default, termination, debarment
- Good performance = stronger past performance = easier to win next contract

**Contract Terms Overview:**
- Every contract has a Statement of Work (SOW) or Performance Work Statement (PWS)
- Delivery schedule, milestones, quality standards
- Reporting requirements, invoicing procedures
- Compliance is non-negotiable—read your contract, understand it, meet it

### Understanding Contract Terms (15 minutes)

**Key Clauses:**

**Statement of Work (SOW) / Performance Work Statement (PWS):**
- Defines what you're delivering
- Tasks, deliverables, acceptance criteria
- Read it line by line—this is your job description
- If something is unclear, ask the contracting officer before you start
- Document any clarifications in writing

**Performance Requirements:**
- What "good" looks like
- Metrics, standards, specifications
- Timeline and milestones
- Quality thresholds
- Know these cold—they're how you'll be evaluated

**Deliverable Specifications:**
- Format (PDF, Word, etc.)
- Content requirements
- Submission method and deadline
- Acceptance process
- Missing a spec can mean rejected deliverable = no payment = default

**Timeline and Milestones:**
- Delivery dates are often firm
- Late delivery can trigger cure notice or default
- Build buffer into your schedule
- Communicate early if you see a risk

**Compliance Obligations:**
- Reporting: monthly reports, progress reports, status updates
- Documentation: maintain records, timesheets, receipts
- Quality: inspections, testing, certifications
- Subcontractor compliance: if you have subs, you're responsible for their compliance too

### Performance Management (20 minutes)

**Meeting Requirements:**

**Deliverable Management:**
- Track every deliverable from day one
- Create internal due dates 3-5 days before contract due date
- Quality review before submission
- Submit in required format, to required recipient
- Get written confirmation of receipt
- Document acceptance—this often triggers payment

**Quality Assurance:**
- Don't submit work you know is subpar
- Internal review process before submission
- Use checklists to verify requirements
- If the government rejects a deliverable, fix it fast and resubmit
- Rejections delay payment and hurt your CPARS

**Timeline Compliance:**
- Late = default risk
- If you're going to be late, notify the contracting officer immediately
- Request modification if scope or timeline needs to change
- Document everything—emails, memos, meeting notes

**CPARS (Contractor Performance Assessment Reporting System):**
- The government rates your performance at least annually
- Ratings: Exceptional, Very Good, Satisfactory, Marginal, Unsatisfactory
- Past performance is a major evaluation factor on future bids
- A bad CPARS follows you for 3-5 years
- Dispute ratings you disagree with—there's a process
- Your goal: Very Good or Exceptional on every dimension

**Managing CPARS:**
- Perform well—that's the foundation
- Document your performance: on-time deliveries, quality, communication
- At end of contract or periodically, the government completes CPARS
- You have the right to comment on the assessment before it's final
- Take that seriously—respond professionally with evidence

### Deliverable Management (12 minutes)

**Deliverable Requirements:**
- Read the SOW for each deliverable
- Format, content, length, submission method
- Who receives it, when, how
- Acceptance criteria—what triggers "accepted"?
- Don't assume—verify

**Managing Deliverables:**
- Create a deliverable tracker: deliverable, due date, status, submission date, acceptance date
- Assign owner for each deliverable
- Review 2 weeks before due, 1 week before, day before
- Submit early when possible—gives time for corrections
- Save confirmation of submission and acceptance

**Acceptance Process:**
- Government reviews deliverable
- May accept, reject, or request revisions
- Rejection = fix and resubmit—don't take it personally, fix it
- Acceptance often triggers invoice/payment
- Document acceptance in writing

### Ongoing Compliance (6 minutes)

**Monitoring and Maintenance:**
- Weekly or monthly compliance check: are we on track?
- Review contract monthly against your performance
- Identify risks early—scope creep, timeline slips, resource issues
- Communicate proactively with contracting officer
- Corrective actions: if you see a problem, fix it before it becomes a default

**Issue Identification:**
- Scope changes? Get a modification
- Can't meet timeline? Notify early, request extension or mod
- Quality issue? Address immediately
- Subcontractor problem? Your responsibility—fix it

**Corrective Actions:**
- Document the issue, root cause, corrective action
- Implement fix, verify it worked
- Report to contracting officer if contractually required
- Learn and prevent recurrence

### Wrap-up (2 minutes)

**Key Takeaways:**
1. Read and understand your contract terms—SOW, deliverables, timelines
2. Track every deliverable; submit on time, in format, meeting specs
3. CPARS matters—perform well, dispute unfair ratings
4. Communicate early if you see problems
5. Compliance is ongoing, not one-time

**Action Items:**
- Create deliverable tracker for your contract
- Schedule monthly compliance reviews
- Establish internal quality check before submission
- Document all submissions and acceptances
