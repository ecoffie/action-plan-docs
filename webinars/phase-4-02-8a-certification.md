**What is the 8(a) Program?**
- Purpose: Assist small disadvantaged companies to excel and expand in the Federal Marketplace
- 9-year program duration
- Business development program, not just a certification
- Requires a lot of paperwork (computer work) to be successful
- Takes time, energy, and smarts

**Program Statistics:**
- Total 8(a) companies: Approximately 6,000
- Newly minted 8(a) companies: ~300 per year
- Concentrated in states like Arizona, Virginia, California
- Significant portion of federal contracting revenue

**Program Overview:**
- 9-year program seems like a lot of time
- Enough time to make your mark in Federal Contracting - if you work smart!
- Two periods: Years 1-4 (Development), Years 5-9 (Growth)
- Graduation based on size standards

### Eligibility Requirements (25 minutes)

**Social Disadvantage:**

**Presumed Groups:**
- African Americans
- Hispanic Americans
- Native Americans (American Indians, Eskimos, Aleuts, or Native Hawaiians)
- Asian Pacific Americans
- Subcontinent Asian Americans
- Other groups as determined by SBA

**Individual Disadvantage Demonstration:**
- If not in presumed group, must demonstrate social disadvantage
- Show bias or discrimination
- Economic disadvantage alone not sufficient
- Documented evidence required
- Case-by-case determination

**Economic Disadvantage:**

**Net Worth Requirements:**
- Net worth less than $850,000 (excluding equity in primary residence and business)
- Assets must be under threshold
- Adjusted for inflation periodically
- Exclusions for primary residence and business equity
- Must demonstrate economic disadvantage

**Income Limits:**
- Adjusted gross income (AGI) averaged over three years
- Thresholds vary but generally lower than $400,000
- Three-year average considered
- Current income limits apply
- Must demonstrate economic disadvantage through income

**Asset Limits:**
- Total assets under $6.5 million
- Excluding primary residence and business equity
- Liquid and non-liquid assets counted
- Fair market value determination
- Must meet asset thresholds

**Adjusted Gross Income:**
- Three-year average AGI considered
- Must be below threshold
- Tax returns reviewed
- Consistent income pattern
- Economic disadvantage demonstrated

**Business Requirements:**

**Small Business Size:**
- Must meet small business size standard for primary NAICS code
- Size standards vary by industry
- Based on employees or revenue
- Must qualify as small business
- Verify size standard before applying

**Personal Financial Interest:**
- Individual claiming disadvantage must own at least 51% of business
- Must show personal financial investment
- Control and ownership required
- Active management expected
- Personal commitment demonstrated

**Business Ownership:**
- Unconditional ownership of at least 51%
- Must be direct ownership
- Cannot be through another entity
- Control and management required
- Ownership structure matters

**Management and Control:**
- Must manage day-to-day operations
- Must control long-term decision-making
- Cannot be controlled by non-disadvantaged individuals
- Active management required
- Control must be demonstrated

**Good Character:**
- Must demonstrate good character
- No criminal convictions (certain types)
- No suspension or debarment
- Business integrity required
- Character assessment conducted

**Potential for Success:**
- Must show potential for success
- Business viability required
- Technical and management capability
- Financial capability
- Reasonable prospect of success

**Additional Requirements:**
- U.S. citizenship (for individual claiming disadvantage)
- Business must be small business
- Business must be for-profit
- Must be in business for at least two years (or show potential)

### Application Process (25 minutes)

**Pre-Application:**

**Eligibility Self-Assessment:**
- Review all eligibility requirements carefully
- Assess social disadvantage status
- Calculate net worth and income
- Verify business size standard
- Determine if you qualify before applying

**Documentation Gathering:**
- Personal financial statements
- Business financial statements
- Tax returns (3 years)
- Business registration documents
- Resumes of key personnel
- Business plan
- Past performance documentation
- Bank statements
- Asset documentation

**Financial Preparation:**
- Organize financial records
- Calculate net worth accurately
- Prepare personal financial statement (SBA Form 413)
- Gather tax returns
- Document all assets and liabilities
- Exclude primary residence and business equity correctly

**Application Steps:**

**SBA Connect Portal:**
- Apply through SBA Connect (certify.sba.gov)
- Online application system
- Create account
- Start application
- Complete all sections

**Application Sections:**
- Business information
- Ownership information
- Social disadvantage narrative
- Economic disadvantage information
- Financial information
- Business capability statement
- Past performance
- Management and control
- Character information

**Required Documentation:**
- Personal financial statement (SBA Form 413)
- Business financial statements
- Tax returns (3 years personal and business)
- Business registration documents
- Articles of incorporation/organization
- Operating agreements
- Resumes
- Business plan
- Past performance documentation
- Other supporting documents

**Financial Disclosures:**
- Complete personal financial statement
- Disclose all assets and liabilities
- Income information
- Bank account information
- Real estate holdings
- Investments
- Other financial interests
- Accurate and complete disclosure required

**Submission:**
- Submit complete application
- All sections completed
- All required documents attached
- Review before submission
- Submit through portal
- Keep confirmation

**Review and Decision:**

**Processing Timeline:**
- Typically 90 days for review
- Can take longer if information requested
- Complex cases may take longer
- Timeline varies by SBA district office
- Be patient but follow up

**Requests for Information:**
- SBA may request additional information
- Respond promptly and completely
- Provide requested documents
- Answer questions thoroughly
- Keep copies of everything submitted

**Site Visit (if required):**
- SBA may conduct site visit
- Interview key personnel
- Review business operations
- Verify information in application
- Assess business capability
- Be prepared and professional

**Approval or Denial:**
- Approval: Welcome to 8(a) program
- Denial: Can appeal or reapply
- Understand reasons for denial
- Address issues before reapplying
- Appeal process available
