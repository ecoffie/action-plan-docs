## Presentation Notes

### Opening (5 minutes)
**Welcome and Phase 3 Context:**
- "Welcome to Phase 3: Business Development. This phase is about building relationships and finding opportunities BEFORE they hit SAM.gov."
- "Today we're focusing on identifying your top 25 buyers - the agencies and offices that buy what you sell."
- "This is proactive business development, not reactive bidding."

**Why Opportunities Exist Before SAM.gov:**
- Government planning happens months/years in advance
- Acquisition planning documents are public
- Forecast lists show upcoming needs
- Relationships give early access
- Sources Sought notices come before RFPs
- Industry days reveal upcoming opportunities

**Proactive vs. Reactive Approach:**
- **Reactive**: Wait for SAM.gov posting, then scramble to respond
- **Proactive**: Identify buyers, build relationships, get involved early
- **Result**: Better positioned, higher win rates, less competition

### Researching Top Buyers (20 minutes)

**Using USAspending.gov:**
- **Agency Spending Data**: See who spends the most in your NAICS codes
- **Historical Spending Patterns**: Understand spending trends over time
- **Top Contractors Analysis**: See who's winning (potential partners or competitors)
- **Spending Trends**: Identify growing vs. declining markets
- **Set-Aside Analysis**: See which agencies use small business set-asides

**Federal Procurement Data System (FPDS):**
- **Contract Award Data**: Detailed award information
- **Agency Spending by NAICS**: Find agencies buying your services/products
- **Award History**: See patterns and trends
- **Competitive Analysis**: Understand the competitive landscape
- **Office ID Information**: Get specific office identifiers for contact research

**Identifying Top 25 Buyers:**

**Ranking Criteria:**
1. **Spending Volume**: How much do they spend in your NAICS codes?
2. **Relevance to Your Business**: Do they buy what you sell?
3. **Agency Mission Alignment**: Does their mission align with your capabilities?
4. **Growth Potential**: Are they growing or maintaining spending?
5. **Set-Aside Usage**: Do they use small business set-asides?
6. **Geographic Presence**: Are they in your service area?
7. **Contract Vehicle Usage**: Do they use IDIQs, GSA Schedules, etc.?

**Building Your Target Market List (TML):**
From GovCon Giants Course - "Are You On The Right Track?"

**TML Components:**
- Agency name
- Office name
- Office ID (from FPDS)
- Spending amount (from USAspending/FPDS)
- NAICS codes they buy
- Contract vehicles they use
- Key contacts (OSDBU, COs)
- Forecast information
- Recent awards
- Strategic fit assessment

**TML Process:**
1. Research agencies using USAspending.gov and FPDS
2. Identify offices within agencies
3. Get Office IDs from FPDS
4. Research spending patterns
5. Find forecast lists
6. Identify contacts
7. Prioritize based on fit and opportunity
8. Build your top 25 list

### Finding Opportunities Before SAM.gov (20 minutes)

**Pre-Solicitation Activities:**

**Sources Sought Notices:**
- Market research tool agencies use
- Posted on SAM.gov (Contract Opportunities)
- No award - just information gathering
- CRITICAL to respond - shows interest and capabilities
- Often leads to set-asides or direct awards
- Response strategy: Professional, capability-focused, relationship-building

**Market Research Requests:**
- Agencies seeking information about capabilities
- Opportunity to showcase your company
- Build relationships with program managers
- Understand upcoming needs
- Position for future opportunities

**RFI Responses:**
- Request for Information
- No award, but valuable for:
  - Relationship building
  - Understanding requirements
  - Positioning capabilities
  - Getting on distribution lists

**Industry Days:**
- Agency-hosted events
- Learn about upcoming opportunities
- Meet contracting officers and program managers
- Network with other contractors
- Understand agency priorities
- Get on mailing lists

**Agency Planning Documents:**

**Acquisition Forecasts:**
- Show planned procurements
- Often 6-12 months in advance
- Include estimated values
- Show NAICS codes
- Available on agency websites
- Updated quarterly or annually

**How to Find Forecast Lists** (from GovCon Giants Course):
- Start at Office level (if you have Office ID)
- If not found, go to Agency level
- If not at Agency level, check national/headquarters level
- Call agency as last resort if forecast not available online
- Forecasts are typically under "Doing Business With" or "Small Business" sections

**Strategic Plans:**
- Agency strategic planning documents
- Show long-term priorities
- Identify growth areas
- Understand mission focus
- Help align your capabilities

**Budget Documents:**
- Show funding priorities
- Identify new programs
- Understand spending increases/decreases
- Help predict opportunities
- Available on agency websites and OMB

**Contract Vehicle Planning:**
- IDIQ planning and awards
- Multiple Award Contract (MAC) planning
- GSA Schedule usage
- Agency-specific vehicles
- Teaming opportunities

**Relationship-Driven Opportunities:**

**Direct Agency Engagement:**
- Proactive outreach to agencies
- Capability briefings
- Understanding needs before solicitation
- Early involvement in acquisition planning
- Shaping requirements (when appropriate)

**Contracting Officer Relationships:**
- Regular communication
- Understanding their priorities
- Providing value and insights
- Being a resource, not just a vendor
- Early notification of opportunities

**Program Manager Relationships:**
- Understanding mission needs
- Technical discussions
- Solution development
- Early involvement
- Trusted advisor role

**Early Involvement Opportunities:**
- Sources Sought responses
- Industry day participation
- RFI responses
- One-on-one meetings
- Advisory roles (when available)

### Agency Procurement Processes (10 minutes)

**Understanding Agency Needs:**

**Agency Missions and Priorities:**
- Each agency has a specific mission
- Understand how your services/products support that mission
- Align your messaging with agency priorities
- Show mission understanding in all communications

**Budget Cycles:**
- Federal fiscal year: October 1 - September 30
- Budget planning: 12-18 months in advance
- Budget execution: During fiscal year
- Understanding cycles helps timing

**Procurement Planning:**
- Agencies plan acquisitions in advance
- Forecasts show planned procurements
- Understanding planning helps positioning
- Early involvement is key

**Contract Vehicle Strategies:**
- IDIQs for recurring needs
- GSA Schedules for commercial items
- Agency-specific vehicles
- Understanding which vehicles agencies use
- Positioning for appropriate vehicles

**Key Decision Makers:**

**Contracting Officers:**
- Make award decisions
- Manage contracts
- Key relationship to build
- Understand their priorities and challenges

**Program Managers:**
- Define requirements
- Use the services/products
- Technical decision makers
- Understand mission needs

**Small Business Specialists:**
- Advocate for small businesses
- Entry point for many agencies
- Resource for opportunities
- Help with set-asides

**End Users:**
- Actually use what you provide
- Understand real needs
- Can influence requirements
- Valuable for understanding requirements
