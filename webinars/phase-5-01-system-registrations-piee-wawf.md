## Presentation Notes

### Opening (5 minutes)
**Welcome and Phase 5 Context:**
- "Welcome to Phase 5: Contract Management. You've won a contract—congratulations. Now you need to get paid and stay compliant. That's where PIEE and WAWF come in."
- "These are the systems the Department of Defense and many other agencies use for contract management, receiving, invoicing, and payment."
- "If you don't register in these systems, you can't submit invoices. And if you can't submit invoices, you don't get paid. Let's fix that today."

**Required Systems Overview:**
- PIEE: Procurement Integrated Enterprise Environment—central hub for DOD contractors
- WAWF: Wide Area Workflow—used for receiving reports and invoice submission
- Many DoD contracts require both
- Civilian agencies may use different systems (we'll touch on that)
- Register early—don't wait until you have deliverables due

**Why Registration is Critical:**
- No registration = no invoice submission = no payment
- Processing can take days or weeks—start before you need it
- Your contracting officer will give you the specifics for your contract
- Missing registration delays payment and can put you in default

### PIEE Registration (20 minutes)

**What is PIEE?**
- Procurement Integrated Enterprise Environment
- DoD's enterprise-wide system for contract management
- Replaces multiple legacy systems
- Single sign-on for many DoD applications
- Used for: contract acceptance, modifications, invoices (in some cases), performance reporting, document access

**Registration Process:**
- Go to piee.eb.mil (or the current PIEE URL—verify with your contracting officer)
- Create a user account with your email
- Register your entity (business) in PIEE
- Link to your SAM.gov/UEI
- Request access to specific modules based on your contract
- Your contracting officer may need to approve or initiate access—ask them
- Approval can take 3-10 business days

**PIEE Functions:**
- **Contract award acceptance**: Electronically accept and sign contracts
- **Modifications**: Review and accept contract modifications (mods)
- **Invoicing**: Some contracts use PIEE for invoice submission
- **Performance reporting**: CPARS, progress reports
- **Document access**: Contract documents, DD forms, technical specs

**Key Point:** Your contracting officer or contract administrator will tell you exactly which PIEE modules you need. Don't guess—ask.

### WAWF Registration (20 minutes)

**What is WAWF?**
- Wide Area Workflow
- DoD's system for receiving and invoicing
- Used to create and submit receiving reports (DD250, etc.)
- Used to create and submit invoices
- Payment is processed through WAWF
- Most DoD contractors use WAWF for invoicing

**Registration Process:**
- Access WAWF through PIEE (single sign-on) or directly at wawf.eb.mil
- Complete WAWF training—often required before access is granted
- Register your business entity
- Set up EFT (Electronic Funds Transfer) if not already in SAM.gov
- Your CAGE code and UEI must match SAM.gov
- Contracting officer may need to add you to the contract in WAWF

**WAWF Functions:**
- **Receiving reports**: Document that work was completed/delivered (DD Form 250)
- **Invoice creation**: Create invoices that link to receiving reports
- **Invoice submission**: Submit for payment
- **Payment tracking**: See status of invoices (submitted, paid)
- **Document management**: Access contract documents

**Critical Flow:** Deliver work → Create receiving report (or contracting officer creates it) → Create invoice linked to receiving report → Submit → Payment typically within 30 days

**Training:**
- WAWF offers free online training
- Complete training before your first invoice
- Saves time and prevents rejections
- Training covers: creating receiving reports, creating invoices, correcting errors

### System Navigation (10 minutes)

**Getting Started:**
- Log in with your PIEE credentials
- Dashboard shows your contracts, pending actions, recent submissions
- Use Help or ? for system-specific guidance
- Contact your contracting officer for contract-specific questions
- WAWF has a robust help section and user guides

**Common Tasks:**
- **Submitting invoices**: Create invoice → Link to receiving report → Submit
- **Creating receiving reports**: If you're responsible, create DD250 after delivery
- **Tracking status**: Check invoice status regularly
- **Accessing documents**: Download contract, mods, specifications

**Common Mistakes to Avoid:**
- Wrong contract or line item number
- Invoice doesn't match receiving report
- Missing required fields
- Wrong banking info (must match SAM.gov)
- Submitting before receiving report is accepted

### Best Practices (3 minutes)

**System Usage:**
- Log in regularly to check status
- Complete all required training
- Keep your SAM.gov info current—WAWF pulls from SAM
- Document everything: save confirmation numbers, screenshots
- If something fails, contact your contracting officer immediately

**Support Resources:**
- Your contracting officer is your first resource
- WAWF help desk for system issues
- PIEE help for access and account issues
- Don't wait until payment is overdue to ask for help

### Wrap-up (2 minutes)

**Key Takeaways:**
1. PIEE and WAWF are required for DoD contract management and payment
2. Register early—before you need to invoice
3. Complete WAWF training
4. Your contracting officer will guide you on your specific contract
5. Keep credentials secure; save all confirmation numbers

**Action Items:**
- Register in PIEE this week if you have a DoD contract or award imminent
- Complete WAWF training
- Confirm with your contracting officer which systems you need
- Verify your SAM.gov and banking info is current
