### Opening (5 minutes)
**Welcome and Context:**
- "Welcome to today's webinar on setting up and attending meetings with government buyers. This is one of the most critical skills in federal contracting - building relationships."
- "Today we'll cover how to find contacts, reach out effectively, prepare for meetings, and conduct productive capability briefings."

**Why Meetings Matter:**
- Relationships are the foundation of federal contracting

- Early involvement in acquisition planning
- Direct access to decision makers
- Understanding real needs vs. stated requirements
- Building trust and credibility

### Finding Contacts (15 minutes)

**Contracting Officers:**
- **Agency Websites**: Most agencies list contracting offices and contacts
- **SAM.gov Points of Contact**: Entity registration shows POCs
- **FPDS Award Data**: Shows who awarded contracts (Office ID)
- **Industry Events**: Meet COs at industry days and conferences
- **Referrals**: Ask Apex Accelerator, other contractors, partners

**Small Business Specialists (OSDBU/OSS):**
This is KEY - Small Business Specialists are your entry point.

**How to Find Small Business Office from Office ID** (from GovCon Giants Course):

**Step 1: Convert Office ID to Government Office Name**
- Copy Office ID from FPDS (Federal Procurement Data System)
- Paste Office ID into SAM.gov search
- Find which Office name correlates to that ID number
- Write down that Office name for your search

**Alternative Method:**
- Copy and paste Office ID into ChatGPT
- Ask it to provide Government Office Name
- Note: Verify on website - check if it's generic or specific contact

**Step 2: Find OSDBU/OSS on Agency Website**
- Visit website of "Government Office Name"
- Search for: 1) OSDBU, 2) SB Liaison, or 3) SB Specialist (OSS)
- Typically under: "Procurement", "Contracting with", or "Doing business with [agency]"

**Step 3: If Not Found at Office Level**
- Go one level higher to the Agency in charge of the office
- Look for OSS listed by Regions/Commands/Areas that covers your Office
- Check agency-level small business office

**Step 4: If Still Not Found**
- Look for any available number to contracting division
- Call and ask for the OSS point of contact (POC)
- If none exists at office level, there should be an OSS at the contract agency level

**Important Note:**
- Some directors will ask that you send your Capability Statement first
- They will forward to the appropriate person
- This is actually a good thing - gets your materials in front of the right people

### Reaching Out (15 minutes)

**Crafting Introduction Emails:**

**Subject Line Best Practices:**
- Be specific: "Capability Briefing Request - [Your Service] for [Agency Name]"
- Include your certification if relevant: "8(a) Small Business - [Service] Capabilities"
- Reference mutual connection if applicable
- Keep it professional and clear

**Email Structure:**
1. **Professional Greeting**: Use proper title (Mr./Ms./Dr.)
2. **Brief Introduction**: Who you are, what your company does
3. **Value Proposition**: What you can do for them (brief, benefit-focused)
4. **Request**: Clear ask - capability briefing, meeting, or information
5. **Call to Action**: Specific next step
6. **Professional Closing**: Contact information, signature

**Email Template Example:**
```
Subject: Capability Briefing Request - [Service] for [Agency]

Dear [Name/Title],

My name is [Your Name], and I'm the [Title] at [Company Name]. We're a [Small Business Type] located in [Location] that specializes in [Your Services].

We understand that [Agency] has needs for [relevant services/products]. We have [X years] of experience providing [services] and have successfully completed [relevant projects/contracts].

We would appreciate the opportunity to schedule a brief capability briefing to discuss how [Company Name] can support [Agency]'s mission and requirements.

Would you be available for a 30-minute meeting in the coming weeks? I'm happy to work around your schedule.

Thank you for your time and consideration.

Best regards,
[Your Name]
[Company Name]
[Phone]
[Email]
[Website]
UEI: [Your UEI]
```

**Following Up:**
- **When**: 5-7 business days if no response
- **How**: Brief, polite follow-up email
- **Persistence**: 2-3 follow-ups maximum, then move on
- **Don't**: Be pushy, call repeatedly, or show frustration

### Preparing for Meetings (12 minutes)

**Capability Briefings - What to Prepare:**

**1. Capability Statement** (one-page version)
- Current and professional
- Relevant to agency needs
- Highlight past performance
- Clear differentiators

**2. Presentation Structure** (if doing formal briefing):
- Company overview (2-3 minutes)
- Core competencies (3-5 minutes)
- Past performance (5-7 minutes)
- How you can help them (3-5 minutes)
- Q&A (remaining time)

**3. Key Messages to Communicate:**
- What you do (clear and specific)
- How you're different (differentiators)
- Proof you can do it (past performance)
- How you can help them (value proposition)

**4. Questions to Ask:**
- "What are your current priorities and needs?"
- "What challenges are you facing?"
- "What opportunities are coming up?"
- "How can we best position ourselves to support you?"
- "Who else should we be talking to?"
- "What's the best way to stay informed about opportunities?"

**5. Materials to Bring:**
- Multiple copies of capability statement
- Business cards
- Notepad for taking notes
- Portfolio of past work (if applicable)
- Questions list
- Calendar for scheduling follow-ups

**Meeting Objectives:**
- **Primary**: Build relationship and understand needs
- **Secondary**: Identify opportunities
- **Tertiary**: Position capabilities
- **Remember**: This is relationship building, not a sales pitch

### Conducting Meetings (10 minutes)

**Meeting Best Practices:**

**Professional Presentation:**
- Dress professionally
- Arrive early
- Be prepared and organized
- Respect their time (stick to scheduled duration)

**Active Listening:**
- Listen more than you talk (80/20 rule)
- Take notes
- Ask clarifying questions
- Show genuine interest in their needs

**Asking Good Questions:**
- Open-ended questions
- Focus on their needs and challenges
- Avoid yes/no questions
- Show you've done your research

**Providing Value:**
- Share relevant insights or information
- Offer to help with resources or connections
- Be a resource, not just a vendor
- Follow through on commitments

**Time Management:**
- Respect the time allocated
- Leave time for Q&A
- Don't overstay your welcome
- Schedule follow-up if needed

**Building Rapport:**
- Professional but personable
- Find common ground
- Show respect for their expertise
- Be authentic and genuine

**Follow-up Commitment:**
- Set clear next steps
- Commit to specific actions
- Get their preferred communication method
- Schedule follow-up if appropriate

### Follow-up (3 minutes)

**After Meetings:**

**Thank You Communication:**
- Send within 24 hours
- Thank them for their time
- Reference specific points from conversation
- Include any promised materials
- Restate next steps

**Material Delivery:**
- Send capability statement if requested
- Provide any additional information promised
- Share relevant resources or insights
- Keep it brief and valuable

**Action Items:**
- Complete any commitments made
- Follow up on opportunities discussed
- Connect with other contacts mentioned
- Stay engaged but not pushy

**Ongoing Engagement:**
- Regular but not excessive communication
- Share relevant updates or information
- Invite to events or webinars
- Maintain relationship even without immediate opportunity

### Additional Tips from GovCon Giants Course

**Target Market List (TML) Connection:**
- Use your TML to identify which offices to contact
- Find forecast lists for respective Office or Agency
- If not found at Office level, go to Agency level
- If not at Agency level, check national level
- Call agency as last resort if forecast not available

**Reserve Calling OSS as Last Resort:**
- The objective of the call with OSS is to setup a capability briefing
- Don't waste the call asking basic questions
- Come prepared to discuss how you can serve them
- Have your capability statement ready to send

**Capture Management OSDBU Activities:**
- OSDBU calls are part of capture management
- These are strategic relationship-building activities
- Track your OSDBU contacts and interactions
- Build a database of contacts and their needs
