## Presentation Notes

### Opening (5 minutes)
**Welcome and Introduction:**
- "Welcome to today's webinar on Apex Accelerators - one of the most valuable free resources available to businesses entering federal contracting."
- "Apex Accelerators, formerly known as PTACs (Procurement Technical Assistance Centers), are part of a nationwide network providing free or low-cost assistance to businesses interested in selling to federal, state, and local governments."
- "Today we'll cover what they are, the benefits they offer, how to find your local one, and most importantly - what to say when you contact them."

**History Context:**
- Apex Accelerators are the new name for PTACs (rebranded in recent years)
- Part of national network through APTAC (Association of Procurement Technical Assistance Centers)
- Over 90 centers nationwide
- Funded through cooperative agreements with Department of Defense

### Benefits Section (20 minutes)

**Free or Low-Cost Services:**
- **One-on-One Counseling**: "This is the gold standard service - you get a dedicated counselor who understands federal contracting. They'll help you navigate SAM.gov registration, understand opportunities, review proposals, and provide personalized guidance."
- **Training Workshops**: "They offer regular workshops on topics like SAM.gov registration, proposal writing, NAICS codes, small business certifications, and more. Many are free or very low cost."
- **Bid Matching**: "Automated system that matches opportunities to your business profile. You'll get email notifications when relevant opportunities are posted. This saves hours of manual searching."
- **Proposal Reviews**: "Before you submit that first proposal, have an Apex Accelerator counselor review it. They'll catch common mistakes, suggest improvements, and help you understand compliance requirements."
- **Market Research**: "They can help you identify which agencies buy what you sell, who the key contracting officers are, and what opportunities are coming down the pipeline."

**Expert Guidance:**
- Counselors are certified procurement professionals
- They stay current on federal contracting regulations and changes
- They've seen hundreds of businesses go through this process
- They know the common pitfalls and how to avoid them

**Access to Resources:**
- Direct relationships with local contracting officers
- Connections to Small Business Specialists at agencies
- Information about industry days and events
- Introductions to potential teaming partners
- Access to templates, guides, and reference materials

**Real-World Impact:**
- Businesses that work with Apex Accelerators have higher win rates
- They avoid costly mistakes early in the process
- They get faster through the learning curve
- They build relationships faster

### Finding Your Local Apex Accelerator (8 minutes)

**APTAC Website - Primary Method:**
- Go to www.aptac-us.org
- Click "Find a PTAC" or locator tool
- Enter your zip code or city
- View contact information, services, and staff
- Each center has different strengths - review what they offer

**Alternative Methods:**
- SBA District Offices often have partnerships
- State Economic Development Offices
- Local Chambers of Commerce
- Business associations in your area

**Important Note:**
- Services vary by location
- Some centers specialize in certain industries
- Contact multiple if you're in a border area
- They serve specific geographic regions

### What to Say - Initial Contact (15 minutes)

**Email Template (from guide):**
```
Subject: New Business Seeking Federal Contracting Assistance

Hello,

My name is [Your Name], and I'm the [Title] at [Company Name]. We're a [Type of Business] located in [City, State].

We're interested in entering the federal contracting marketplace and understand that Apex Accelerator provides assistance to businesses like ours.

We're particularly interested in:
- Understanding SAM.gov registration
- Finding opportunities in [your industry/NAICS code]
- Learning about proposal development

Could we schedule an initial consultation to discuss how Apex Accelerator might be able to help us?

Thank you for your time.

Best regards,
[Your Name]
[Company Name]
[Contact Information]
```

**Phone Call Tips:**
- Be professional and concise
- State your name, company, and location
- Mention you're new to federal contracting
- Ask about scheduling an initial consultation
- Don't try to get all your questions answered on the first call

**What to Bring to First Meeting:**
- Business information (EIN, business structure)
- NAICS codes if you've identified them
- Brief description of what you do
- List of questions or areas where you need help
- Capability statement if you have one (even if draft)
- Business cards
- Notepad for taking notes

**Questions to Ask:**
1. "What services does your Apex Accelerator provide?"
2. "How often can we meet with a counselor?"
3. "Do you offer bid matching services?"
4. "Can you help with proposal reviews?"
5. "What training workshops do you offer?"
6. "How do you help businesses connect with contracting officers?"
7. "What should be our first steps in federal contracting?"
8. "Are there any fees for your services?"

**What They'll Ask You:**
- Business description and what you do
- Current status (registered in SAM.gov? Have past performance?)
- Your goals for federal contracting
- Timeline expectations
- Specific challenges or questions

### Maximizing the Relationship (8 minutes)

**Do's:**
- ✅ Come prepared to meetings - bring questions, documents, be ready to discuss
- ✅ Be open and honest about your challenges - they've heard it all
- ✅ Follow through on action items - shows you're serious
- ✅ Attend training and workshops - shows engagement
- ✅ Provide feedback on services - helps them serve you better
- ✅ Share successes and challenges - helps them tailor guidance

**Don'ts:**
- ❌ Don't expect them to write proposals for you - they'll review and guide
- ❌ Don't rely solely on them - you need to be proactive
- ❌ Don't be passive - take initiative, do your homework
- ❌ Don't miss appointments without notice - be professional
- ❌ Don't ignore their advice - they're experts for a reason

**Building the Relationship:**
- Regular communication (monthly check-ins are typical)
- Attend events they host or recommend
- Refer other businesses (if appropriate)
- Share your progress and wins
- Be patient - building federal contracting business takes time
