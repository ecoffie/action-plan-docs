### Understanding Credit Requirements (15 minutes)

**Why Credit is Needed:**

**Bonding Requirements:**
- Many federal contracts require bonds
- Construction contracts often require bonding
- Service contracts may require bonds
- Bonding demonstrates financial capability
- Protects government and subcontractors

**Financial Stability:**
- Shows you can perform contracts
- Demonstrates business viability
- Reduces risk for government
- Builds confidence in your capability
- Required for larger contracts

**Cash Flow Management:**
- Federal contracts can have payment delays
- Need working capital to perform work
- Pay subcontractors before payment received
- Manage expenses during contract performance
- Cash flow is critical

**Contract Performance:**
- Need resources to start work
- Equipment and materials purchases
- Payroll before payment received
- Performance requires upfront investment
- Credit enables performance

**Types of Credit/Bonding:**

**Bid Bonds:**
- Required for many solicitations
- Guarantee you'll enter contract if awarded
- Typically 5-20% of bid amount
- Returned if not awarded
- Required for many construction and service contracts

**Performance Bonds:**
- Guarantee contract performance
- Protect government if you default
- Typically 100% of contract value
- Required for many contracts over $150,000
- Construction contracts commonly require

**Payment Bonds:**
- Protect subcontractors and suppliers
- Ensure payment to subs and suppliers
- Required with performance bonds
- Protects your supply chain
- Required for construction contracts

**Lines of Credit:**
- Working capital for operations
- Bridge payment delays
- Cash flow management
- Flexible borrowing
- Essential for contract performance

**Surety Bonds:**
- Various contract requirements
- License and permit bonds
- Custom bonds for specific needs
- Bonding capacity determines contract size
- Relationship with surety important

### Building Business Credit (15 minutes)

**Credit Building Basics:**

**Establish Business Credit Separate from Personal:**
- Business credit file separate from personal
- Build business credit history
- Don't rely solely on personal credit
- Establish business credit profile
- Important for larger contracts

**Business Credit Accounts:**
- Business credit cards
- Business loans
- Trade credit with suppliers
- Vendor accounts
- Build credit history

**Timely Payment History:**
- Pay all bills on time
- Build positive payment history
- Consistent on-time payments
- Most important credit factor
- Takes time to build

**Credit Utilization:**
- Don't max out credit lines
- Keep utilization under 30%
- Shows responsible credit use
- Improves credit scores
- Demonstrates financial discipline

**Financial Documentation:**

**Financial Statements:**
- **Profit & Loss (P&L)**: Revenue and expenses
- **Balance Sheet**: Assets and liabilities
- **Cash Flow Statement**: Cash inflows and outflows
- Required for credit applications
- Show financial health

**Tax Returns:**
- Business tax returns (last 2-3 years)
- Show revenue and profitability
- Demonstrate financial stability
- Required for many applications
- Official financial record

**Bank Statements:**
- Last 3-6 months of statements
- Show cash flow patterns
- Demonstrate account activity
- Required for applications
- Show financial activity

**Accounts Receivable Aging:**
- Outstanding invoices
- Payment patterns
- Cash flow timing
- Required for some applications
- Shows incoming revenue

**Projected Financials:**
- Forecast for coming year
- Show growth plans
- Demonstrate potential
- Required for some applications
- Show business planning

**Credit Scores and Reports:**

**Business Credit Bureaus:**
- **Dun & Bradstreet (D&B)**: Most important for federal contracting
- **Experian Business**: Business credit reporting
- **Equifax Business**: Business credit information
- Monitor all three
- Build profiles with each

**Credit Score Improvement:**
- Pay bills on time (most important)
- Keep credit utilization low
- Build credit history over time
- Monitor credit reports regularly
- Dispute errors immediately

**Monitoring Credit Reports:**
- Check reports regularly
- Identify issues early
- Track credit building progress
- Understand your credit profile
- Fix errors promptly

**Dispute Resolution:**
- Dispute errors on credit reports
- Provide documentation
- Follow up on disputes
- Keep records of disputes
- Protect your credit profile

### Vendor/Supplier Credit Applications (12 minutes)

**Preparing Applications:**

**Required Documentation:**
- Business registration documents
- Financial statements (P&L, Balance Sheet)
- Tax returns (last 2-3 years)
- Bank statements (last 3-6 months)
- Business credit references
- Personal guarantees (may be required)

**Financial Information:**
- Annual revenue
- Profit margins
- Cash flow information
- Accounts receivable
- Accounts payable
- Debt obligations

**Business Information:**
- Business name and legal structure
- Years in business
- Number of employees
- Industry/NAICS codes
- Business address
- Contact information

**References:**
- Trade references (suppliers)
- Bank references
- Customer references
- Business references
- Professional references
- Credit references

**Credit Application Process:**

**Where to Apply:**
- Banks (business banking relationships)
- Credit unions
- Online lenders
- Surety companies (for bonding)
- Equipment financing companies
- Trade credit with suppliers

**Application Review:**
- Credit checks (business and personal)
- Financial analysis
- Reference checks
- Business evaluation
- Risk assessment
- Decision process

**Approval Process:**
- Underwriting review
- Credit committee approval
- Terms negotiation
- Documentation requirements
- Approval or denial decision
- Timeframe varies (days to weeks)

**Terms and Conditions:**
- Credit limit
- Interest rates
- Payment terms
- Fees and charges
- Collateral requirements
- Personal guarantees
