# Webinar: Get on Supplier List for Top 25 Federal Suppliers

**Phase:** Phase 3 - Business Development (Repeat)  
**Duration:** 45-60 minutes  
**Level:** Intermediate  
**Prerequisites:** Understanding of subcontracting

## Learning Objectives
By the end of this webinar, participants will be able to:
- Identify top federal contractors in their industry
- Understand supplier diversity programs
- Register with prime contractor supplier portals
- Position themselves for subcontracting opportunities
- Build relationships with prime contractors

## Agenda

### Introduction (5 minutes)
- Welcome and context
- Subcontracting opportunities
- Prime contractor relationships

### Identifying Top Federal Suppliers (15 minutes)
- **Finding Top Contractors**
  - USAspending.gov analysis
  - Top contractors by NAICS
  - Relevant to your industry
  - Growth opportunities
  
- **Top 25 Federal Suppliers**
  - Major primes
  - Industry leaders
  - IDIQ holders
  - Relevant contractors

### Supplier Diversity Programs (15 minutes)
- **Understanding Programs**
  - Supplier diversity focus
  - Small business goals
  - Certification preferences
  - Program benefits
  
- **Registration Process**
  - Supplier portals
  - Registration requirements
  - Profile completion
  - Certification upload

### Building Prime Relationships (12 minutes)
- **Outreach Strategies**
  - Initial contact
  - Capability presentations
  - Relationship building
  - Regular engagement
  
- **Positioning**
  - Unique capabilities
  - Past performance
  - Team contribution
  - Value proposition

### Wrap-up & Next Steps (3 minutes)
- Key takeaways
- Action items
- Next webinar: Monitor Contract Awards

## Key Takeaways
1. Top primes offer significant subcontracting opportunities
2. Supplier diversity programs facilitate access
3. Registration is first step; relationships are key
4. Demonstrate unique value to primes
5. Ongoing engagement maintains visibility
