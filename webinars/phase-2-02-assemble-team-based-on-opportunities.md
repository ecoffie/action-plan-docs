### Team Formation Strategies (15 minutes)

**Prime vs. Subcontractor Positioning:**

**When to Be Prime:**
- You have majority of capabilities
- You want to control the contract
- You can manage the team
- You have past performance as prime
- Strategic growth opportunity

**When to Be Subcontractor:**
- You lack some capabilities
- Larger company is better positioned
- Learning opportunity
- Lower risk
- Building past performance

**Positioning Strategy:**
- Strategic decision based on opportunity
- Consider your goals
- Consider your capabilities
- Consider risk tolerance
- Consider growth objectives

**Risk Considerations:**
- Prime has more risk and responsibility
- Subcontractor has less risk but less control
- Consider your risk tolerance
- Consider your financial capacity
- Consider your experience level

**Teaming Structures:**

**Prime-Subcontractor:**
- Most common structure
- Prime leads, subs support
- Clear hierarchy
- Prime manages contract
- Subcontractors perform work

**Joint Ventures:**
- Two or more companies join together
- Shared ownership and control
- Combined capabilities
- Shared risk and reward
- More complex structure

**Mentor-Protégé Teams:**
- SBA mentor-protégé program
- Large mentor, small protégé
- Protégé learns from mentor
- Combined capabilities
- Protégé gains experience

**Strategic Partnerships:**
- Long-term relationships
- Multiple opportunities
- Complementary capabilities
- Mutual benefit
- Ongoing partnership

**Selecting Team Roles:**

**Lead/Prime Responsibilities:**
- Overall contract management
- Primary point of contact
- Financial responsibility
- Performance accountability
- Team coordination

**Subcontractor Roles:**
- Specific work performance
- Technical expertise
- Past performance contribution
- Capability contribution
- Support prime's effort

**Work Share Allocation:**
- How much work does each do?
- Prime typically does majority
- Subcontractors do specific portions
- Clear work allocation
- Defined responsibilities

**Risk Allocation:**
- Who bears what risk?
- Prime typically bears most risk
- Subcontractors bear their work risk
- Defined in teaming agreement
- Clear risk allocation
